The story

They offered me
Ozempic.
I chose the
Grand Canyon.

I'm a chef. I built my career around food. And then my own body became the patient, and the prescription pad came out. Ozempic. Statins. The whole cascade.

At the Grand Canyon, standing at the rim, something shifted. The human body was not designed to depend on synthetic molecules for its basic function. It was designed to thrive — given real food, clean ingredients, and the space to do what it already knows how to do.

"Real food is not complicated. The food industry made it complicated. There's a difference."

— Idan, Chef & Founder

The turning point

The Grand Canyon moment

Standing at the edge of something vast, I understood that the human body has the same capacity for renewal. The canyon was shaped by water and time, without interference. Your body works the same way — given the right ingredients and the right environment, it does the rest.

I flew home, cleared my kitchen, and built the method I now bring to families across Palm Beach County.

The pantry audit

Five ingredients
found in most kitchens.
Here's what we replace them with.

When I walk into a kitchen, the same five ingredients come up again and again — each one with a cleaner, better-tasting alternative. Reading a label isn't hard once you know what to look for.

Morning · Coffee creamer
Conventional creamer
✦ We use: oat or coconut-based
Most creamers are built on refined seed oils and corn-derived sweeteners. The swap costs the same, tastes better, and starts your morning with ingredients that were designed to be food.
Midday · Salad dressing
Bottled dressing
✦ We use: avocado or olive oil base
A salad is a good choice. The dressing is where most of the benefit gets undone. Avocado oil dressings — widely available, kosher-certified — change that completely.
Evening · The bread basket
Supermarket bread
✦ We use: four-ingredient loaves
Bread needs flour, water, yeast, and salt. Most supermarket loaves have twenty-three ingredients. The clean version is on the same shelf — it just takes knowing which one to reach for.
Everywhere · Added sweeteners
High-fructose corn syrup
✦ We use: whole fruit sweeteners, honey
It appears under 56 different names on ingredient labels. Ketchup, yogurt, granola bars. Replacing it doesn't mean giving up sweetness — it means choosing sweetness that your body recognizes.
Pantry staples · Preservation
Synthetic preservatives
✦ We use: naturally preserved products
BHA, BHT, and TBHQ are petroleum-derived compounds that extend shelf life. Clean alternatives — preserved with vitamin E, rosemary extract, or nothing at all — are just as widely available.
The approach
One swap at a time.
You don't need to overhaul everything at once. The Clean Method starts with the next product you pick up. Momentum matters more than perfection — and the first swap is always the easiest.
